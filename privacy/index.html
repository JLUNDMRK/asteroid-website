Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 15, 2026

Asteroid Audiobook Player is an ad-free hobby project. We respect your privacy and believe you should retain ownership of your data. Asteroid does not run any servers and does not collect personal information, account data, or the titles of your audiobooks. Your library stays on your device and in the cloud folders you choose. The app does not sell your personal data.

1. Local storage on your device

To support playback, offline listening, and restoring app state, Asteroid stores cached files on your device in storage areas provided by Android for the app. This may include downloaded audiobook files, metadata, cover images, playback progress, bookmarks, and related app data.

This data stays on your device unless you use optional cloud backup in your own cloud account (see below). Nothing is sent to Asteroid—there is no Asteroid backend to receive it.

2. Cloud library

Asteroid is built around a cloud audiobook library. You connect a cloud storage account (such as Dropbox or OneDrive) and choose a folder for your library. The app indexes, reads, and downloads only the files and folders you select. Additional cloud providers may be supported in the future.

Audio, metadata, cover images, playback progress, and bookmarks may be downloaded and cached on your device for playback and offline use. Asteroid does not scan your device for audiobook files outside the cloud locations you choose.

Credentials and tokens stay on your device and are sent only to your cloud provider's official APIs. They are never sent to an Asteroid server—Asteroid does not operate one.

3. Open Library and other metadata services

During library scan, Asteroid matches your selected folders to public book records using Open Library. Queries are sent as anonymous title and author text derived from your folder names and ID3 tags—they do not include your name, email, or cloud account. Like any network request, Open Library's API may see your IP address.

Metadata and cover references returned by Open Library are stored in your local index on your device so the app does not repeat the same lookups every time you browse. You are responsible for using Open Library data in accordance with Open Library's terms and policies.

When Open Library does not provide a confident match, the app may optionally query Google Books (where enabled). Use of Google Books data must comply with Google Books API terms.

4. Analytics and crash reporting

To keep the app stable, anonymous crash reports and usage statistics may be sent via Google Firebase Crashlytics and Firebase Analytics—for example aggregated listening time, approximate library size, and non-fatal file read errors. Reports can include device model and OS version. This data cannot be linked to you, is not used for advertising, and is never sold.

5. Supporter contributions (Google Play)

Optional Supporter Status is handled through Google Play Billing (monthly supporter subscription and/or one-time supporter contribution). Payment processing is performed by Google; we do not receive your payment card details.

6. Children's privacy

The app does not knowingly collect personal information from children. It is designed to work with files and accounts you control.
