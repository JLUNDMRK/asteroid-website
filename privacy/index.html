Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 27, 2026

Asteroid Audiobook Player is an ad-free hobby project. We respect your privacy and believe you should retain ownership of your data. The app does not sell your personal data.

1. Local storage and file access

Asteroid may access folders you explicitly select using Android's Storage Access Framework. The app indexes, reads, and plays media only from folders you choose. File contents and folder indexes are not collected or sent to our servers.

2. Google Drive (optional)

You may optionally sign in with Google and pick a Drive folder for your library, or use optional cloud backup in a private app-data area on your own Drive account.

  • drive.file — read and download files only inside folders you pick.
  • drive.appdata — optional backup of listening progress, bookmarks, and metadata JSON in your private app-data folder.

Credentials and tokens stay on your device and are sent only to Google's official APIs. We do not receive your Google credentials or file contents on our own servers.

3. Public metadata lookups

During library scan, Asteroid may query public services such as Open Library (and Google Books in some builds) using book title and author text derived from your folders and tags. Queries are anonymous and do not include personal identifiers beyond what is required for a network request (such as your IP address).

4. Crash reporting

Anonymous crash reports may be sent via Google Firebase Crashlytics to improve stability. Reports can include device model, OS version, and stack traces. They are not used for advertising.

5. In-app contributions (tip jar)

Optional Supporter Status is handled through Google Play Billing. Payment processing is performed by Google; we do not receive your payment card details.

6. Children's privacy

The app does not knowingly collect personal information from children. It is designed to work with files and accounts you control.

7. Contact

Questions about this policy: asteroid@jlundmark.org or an issue on the GitHub repository.
