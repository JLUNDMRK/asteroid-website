2. Intended use

Asteroid is designed for people who build and manage their own audiobook library. The App does not provide audiobooks. It helps organize, browse, and play audio files you already own or control—such as DRM-free purchases, audiobook CDs, and public-domain recordings.

You are responsible for ensuring you have the right to listen to the audiobooks you play.

The App requires a cloud storage account where you keep your files (such as Dropbox or OneDrive). When you connect a cloud provider or use public metadata services (Open Library and, where enabled, Google Books), the App acts as a client on your behalf. You are responsible for complying with those services' terms and any rights required to access your files and metadata.

3. Open Library and metadata

Asteroid extracts title and author candidates from folder names, file names, and embedded tags on the device. Selected search terms may be sent from your device to Open Library (and similar providers where enabled) to retrieve matching book information and covers. File contents, your name or email, and cloud credentials are not sent. Open Library may receive each search query plus technical request data such as your IP address. Over time, many queries from the same IP could suggest which books were looked up—this is not the same as knowing your identity, but it is not fully anonymous.

Metadata downloaded to your device from Open Library must be handled in accordance with Open Library's terms and policies. See also the Privacy Policy.
